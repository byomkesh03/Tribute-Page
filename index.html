Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose

The leader who ignited the flame of freedom

Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose, a prominent leader in India's struggle for independence, meeting Adolf Hitler.

Subhas Chandra Bose, born on January 23, 1897, in Cuttack, Odisha, was a significant figure in India's fight against British rule. He was known for his militant approach to independence and led the Indian National Army (INA) during World War II. His efforts were instrumental in inspiring many to join the freedom movement. Bose's legacy continues to influence Indian politics and society.

Timeline of Netaji's Life:

  • 1897: Born in Cuttack, Odisha.
  • 1919: Graduated from the University of Calcutta with a degree in philosophy.
  • 1920: Passed the Indian Civil Services Examination in England but resigned to join India's freedom movement.
  • 1921: Met Mahatma Gandhi and joined the Indian National Congress.
  • 1938: Elected President of the Indian National Congress.
  • 1941: Escaped house arrest and traveled to Germany to seek support against British rule.
  • 1943: Took command of the Indian National Army in Singapore.
  • 1945: Allegedly died in a plane crash in Taiwan; circumstances of his death remain a subject of controversy.
